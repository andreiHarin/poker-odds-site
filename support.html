Frequently asked questions

What is Poker Odds?

Poker Odds is an offline equity calculator and training app for Texas Hold'em poker. It computes how often a given hand wins at showdown against 2–10 opponents using Monte Carlo simulation, and includes practice modes to train your intuition.

Does the app involve real money or gambling?

No. Poker Odds is purely educational. It contains no real-money play, no virtual chips, no betting, no wagering, and no in-game currency. It does not connect to online poker rooms or casino services. It is a probability calculator — the equivalent of a scientific calculator for poker math.

How does the Monte Carlo simulation work?

Given your two hole cards and any visible community cards, the remaining cards in the deck are unknown. The app deals out random opponent hands and missing community cards thousands of times, evaluates the best 5-of-7 hand for everyone, and counts how often you would win or split the pot. Your equity is the average share of the pot you'd win across all those simulations.

More iterations means a more precise estimate (the error shrinks like 1/√N). The app defaults to a balance between speed and precision, but you can adjust this.

How is this different from GTO solvers?

Poker Odds answers a single question: how often do I win at showdown? It does not tell you whether to bet, call, or fold.

GTO solvers (PioSolver, GTO Wizard, GTO+) compute unexploitable mixed strategies using Counterfactual Regret Minimization. They take ranges, stack sizes, pot, and bet sizings as input, and output action frequencies for an entire decision tree. They are powerful but require setup and substantial compute time. Poker Odds is the calculator that sits at the leaves of every solver's tree — extracted into a tool simple enough to run instantly on your phone.

Does the app work offline?

Yes — completely. Poker Odds makes no network requests at any time. You can use it in airplane mode, with no Wi-Fi, on a flight, or anywhere else.

What data does the app collect?

None. Poker Odds collects no personal data, has no analytics, no ads, no third-party SDKs, and no account system. Your hands, board cards, and exercise scores never leave your device. See the Privacy Policy for the formal version.

Why does the app require iOS 26?

The app uses APIs introduced in iOS 26 for layout and concurrency. If you're running an older iOS version, the App Store will not let you install it. A future update may lower the minimum requirement to broaden compatibility.

The equity number changes a little each time. Is that a bug?

No — it's a feature of Monte Carlo simulation. Because each run uses a different random sample of cards, results vary slightly run to run. The variation gets smaller the more iterations you allow. For most spots, ±0.5% across runs is normal and not worth worrying about.

Can I assign specific cards to opponents?

Yes. In Manual mode you can assign known hole cards to specific opponents and see how that shifts your equity. Any opponents you don't assign will be treated as holding random cards from the remaining deck.
